Facts about Emily Brontë

  • Emily Brontë is most well-known for her novel, Wuthering Heights, but she was also a poet.
  • She was born on 30 July 1818 in Thornton, West Riding of Yorkshire (today known as West Yorkshire).
  • She spent most of her life in the village of Haworth, which is also in West Yorksire.
  • In 1842, Emily and her sister Charlotte went to live in Brussels while they attended school to improve their French and German.
  • Emily was said to be shy and reserved.
  • She has often been described as a lover of nature.
  • Emily died from consumption (now known as tuberculosis) in 1848, meaning she never got to know of the success of her novel, Wuthering Heights.

You can visit the home of the Brontë sisters at the Old Parsonage in Haworth. There are also many public footpaths that allow you to explore the dramatic countryside that inspired their works.
